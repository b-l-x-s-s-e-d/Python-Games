# Vocabulary List (3 Tiers)

## Tier 1 – Most Important

- [ ] **Superfluous** – more than needed; unnecessary.  
  Synonyms: unnecessary, redundant, excessive  
  Example: The teacher removed the superfluous details from the essay.

- [ ] **Paucity** – a small amount; shortage.  
  Synonyms: scarcity, lack  
  Example: There was a paucity of evidence.

- [ ] **Morose** – gloomy or miserable.  
  Synonyms: melancholy, sullen  
  Example: He sat in a morose silence.

- [ ] **Pragmatic** – practical and realistic.  
  Synonyms: sensible, practical  
  Example: He took a pragmatic approach.

- [ ] **Quixotic** – unrealistic and idealistic.  
  Synonyms: dreamy, impractical  
  Example: His quixotic dream was impossible.

- [ ] **Obstinate** – stubborn.  
  Synonyms: inflexible, rigid  
  Example: He remained obstinate despite advice.

- [ ] **Inexorable** – impossible to stop.  
  Synonyms: relentless, unavoidable  
  Example: Time is inexorable.

- [ ] **Ambiguous** – unclear or confusing.  
  Synonyms: vague, uncertain  
  Example: The instructions were ambiguous.

- [ ] **Diligent** – hardworking and careful.  
  Synonyms: industrious, thorough  
  Example: She is a diligent student.

- [ ] **Elusive** – hard to find or catch.  
  Synonyms: evasive, tricky  
  Example: The truth remained elusive.

- [ ] **Inevitable** – certain to happen.  
  Synonyms: unavoidable, certain  
  Example: Change is inevitable.

- [ ] **Meticulous** – very detailed.  
  Synonyms: precise, careful  
  Example: He is meticulous with work.

- [ ] **Resilient** – able to recover.  
  Synonyms: tough, strong  
  Example: Children are resilient.

- [ ] **Oblivious** – unaware.  
  Synonyms: ignorant, unmindful  
  Example: He was oblivious to the noise.

- [ ] **Altruistic** – caring for others.  
  Synonyms: selfless, generous  
  Example: Her altruistic nature helped many.

- [ ] **Conscientious** – careful and responsible.  
  Synonyms: diligent, reliable  
  Example: She is a conscientious worker.

- [ ] **Indifferent** – not caring.  
  Synonyms: unconcerned, apathetic  
  Example: He was indifferent to the outcome.

- [ ] **Latent** – hidden or inactive.  
  Synonyms: dormant, concealed  
  Example: She had latent talent.

- [ ] **Formidable** – powerful and intimidating.  
  Synonyms: strong, imposing  
  Example: He was a formidable opponent.

- [ ] **Repudiate** – to reject or refuse.  
  Synonyms: deny, disown  
  Example: He repudiated the false accusation.

- [ ] **Abate** – to become less intense.  
  Synonyms: diminish, subside  
  Example: The storm began to abate.

- [ ] **Rebuke** – to scold or criticise.  
  Synonyms: reprimand, scold  
  Example: She rebuked him for lying.

- [ ] **Resentment** – bitter anger.  
  Synonyms: grudge, bitterness  
  Example: Resentment grew over time.

- [ ] **Contrition** – feeling guilty.  
  Synonyms: remorse, regret  
  Example: She showed contrition for her mistake.

## Tier 2 – Very Useful

- [ ] **Meagre** – very small or not enough.  
  Synonyms: scant, sparse, inadequate  
  Example: He survived on a meagre amount of food.

- [ ] **Paltry** – so small it seems worthless.  
  Synonyms: trivial, insignificant, pitiful  
  Example: She was insulted by the paltry reward.

- [ ] **Zealous** – very enthusiastic or passionate.  
  Synonyms: eager, fervent, devoted  
  Example: The zealous fans waited overnight for tickets.

- [ ] **Apathy** – lack of interest or emotion.  
  Synonyms: indifference, detachment  
  Example: His apathy towards school worried his parents.

- [ ] **Vivacity** – liveliness and energy.  
  Synonyms: vitality, enthusiasm  
  Example: Her vivacity lit up the classroom.

- [ ] **Vivacious** – lively and energetic.  
  Synonyms: spirited, bubbly  
  Example: She had a vivacious personality.

- [ ] **Delirious** – extremely excited or confused.  
  Synonyms: frenzied, ecstatic  
  Example: He was delirious with joy after winning.

- [ ] **Malevolent** – wishing harm on others.  
  Synonyms: malicious, evil  
  Example: The villain had a malevolent smile.

- [ ] **Mandate** – official order or command.  
  Synonyms: decree, instruction  
  Example: The government issued a new mandate.

- [ ] **Emaciated** – extremely thin and weak.  
  Synonyms: gaunt, skeletal  
  Example: The emaciated dog needed care.

- [ ] **Penultimate** – second last.  
  Synonyms: next-to-last  
  Example: This is the penultimate chapter.

- [ ] **Homely** – plain or unattractive.  
  Synonyms: plain, dull  
  Example: The homely house lacked charm.

- [ ] **Chivalrous** – polite and honourable.  
  Synonyms: gallant, courteous  
  Example: He was chivalrous to his guests.

- [ ] **Perspicacity** – sharp understanding.  
  Synonyms: insight, perception  
  Example: Her perspicacity impressed the teacher.

- [ ] **Loquacious** – very talkative.  
  Synonyms: chatty, verbose  
  Example: The loquacious student never stopped talking.

- [ ] **Gregarious** – social and friendly.  
  Synonyms: outgoing, sociable  
  Example: He is gregarious at parties.

- [ ] **Nefarious** – evil or criminal.  
  Synonyms: wicked, villainous  
  Example: He planned a nefarious scheme.

- [ ] **Melancholy** – deep sadness.  
  Synonyms: gloom, sorrow  
  Example: A feeling of melancholy filled the room.

- [ ] **Desolate** – empty and sad.  
  Synonyms: barren, abandoned  
  Example: The town looked desolate.

- [ ] **Dilapidated** – badly damaged.  
  Synonyms: rundown, crumbling  
  Example: The dilapidated building was unsafe.

- [ ] **Ethereal** – unreal and delicate.  
  Synonyms: heavenly, ghostly  
  Example: The music was ethereal.

- [ ] **Euphoria** – extreme happiness.  
  Synonyms: joy, ecstasy  
  Example: He felt euphoria after winning.

- [ ] **Luminescent** – softly glowing.  
  Synonyms: radiant, glowing  
  Example: The jellyfish was luminescent.

- [ ] **Vociferous** – loud and forceful.  
  Synonyms: noisy, outspoken  
  Example: The crowd was vociferous.

- [ ] **Imperious** – bossy and arrogant.  
  Synonyms: domineering, arrogant  
  Example: His imperious tone annoyed everyone.

- [ ] **Flout** – openly disobey.  
  Synonyms: defy, ignore  
  Example: He flouted the rules.

- [ ] **Pernicious** – very harmful.  
  Synonyms: damaging, destructive  
  Example: The pernicious habit ruined his health.

## Tier 3 – Good to Know

- [ ] **Grimaced** – showed pain or disgust on the face.  
  Synonyms: winced, scowled  
  Example: He grimaced when he tasted the medicine.

- [ ] **Stern** – serious and strict.  
  Synonyms: severe, firm, harsh  
  Example: The stern principal demanded silence.

- [ ] **Serpentine** – snake-like or winding.  
  Synonyms: twisting, sinuous  
  Example: The serpentine road was hard to follow.

- [ ] **Blithesome** – cheerful and carefree.  
  Synonyms: joyful, merry  
  Example: She gave a blithesome laugh.

- [ ] **Gluttony** – excessive eating or greed.  
  Synonyms: overindulgence, greed  
  Example: His gluttony caused health problems.

- [ ] **Obtrusive** – noticeable and annoying.  
  Synonyms: intrusive, pushy  
  Example: The ads were obtrusive.

- [ ] **Effervescent** – full of energy.  
  Synonyms: lively, sparkling  
  Example: His effervescent mood was contagious.

- [ ] **Corpulent** – very fat.  
  Synonyms: obese, overweight  
  Example: The corpulent cat could not jump.

- [ ] **Emasculate** – weaken or make powerless.  
  Synonyms: undermine, weaken  
  Example: The defeat emasculated the team.

- [ ] **Impudent** – rude and disrespectful.  
  Synonyms: insolent, cheeky  
  Example: His impudent reply shocked her.

- [ ] **Vilify** – speak badly about.  
  Synonyms: slander, insult  
  Example: The media vilified the actor.

- [ ] **Camaraderie** – friendship and trust.  
  Synonyms: fellowship, bonding  
  Example: There was great camaraderie in the team.

- [ ] **Facetious** – joking at the wrong time.  
  Synonyms: flippant, joking  
  Example: His facetious comment annoyed her.

- [ ] **Lament** – express sadness.  
  Synonyms: mourn, grieve  
  Example: They lamented the loss.

- [ ] **Lethargy** – lack of energy.  
  Synonyms: tiredness, sluggishness  
  Example: He struggled with lethargy.

- [ ] **Flippant** – not taking seriously.  
  Synonyms: casual, disrespectful  
  Example: Her flippant attitude upset him.

- [ ] **Eccentricity** – unusual behaviour.  
  Synonyms: oddity, weirdness  
  Example: His eccentricity made him unique.

- [ ] **Animosity** – strong hatred.  
  Synonyms: hostility, hatred  
  Example: There was animosity between rivals.

- [ ] **Verminous** – filthy or disgusting.  
  Synonyms: vile, rotten  
  Example: The verminous alley stank.

- [ ] **Cataclysmic** – extremely disastrous.  
  Synonyms: devastating, catastrophic  
  Example: The earthquake was cataclysmic.
